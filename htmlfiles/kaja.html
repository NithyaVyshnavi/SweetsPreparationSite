Kaja

Kaja is a traditional layered Indian sweet, popular in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. It’s made by folding and deep-frying thin layers of dough, then soaking them in sugar syrup. Its flaky, crispy texture with a sweet coating makes it a delightful treat.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • Water (as needed to knead dough)
  • 2 tbsp rice flour (for layering)
  • 1.5 cups sugar
  • 3/4 cup water (for syrup)
  • Cardamom powder (optional)
  • Oil for deep frying

Preparation Steps

  1. Mix flour and ghee, add water gradually and knead into a soft dough. Rest for 20 minutes.
  2. Roll the dough into thin sheets. Apply a paste of ghee and rice flour between layers and roll it tightly like a log.
  3. Cut the rolled dough into 1-inch pieces and flatten slightly.
  4. Heat oil and deep-fry on medium flame until golden and crispy.
  5. Meanwhile, prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water until it reaches one-string consistency. Add cardamom powder if using.
  6. Dip the fried kajas in warm sugar syrup and let them soak for a few minutes.
  7. Remove and let them cool. Serve once syrup sets slightly.
