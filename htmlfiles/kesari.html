Kesari

Kesari, also known as Sooji Halwa or Rava Kesari, is a popular Indian dessert made from semolina (rava), sugar, ghee, and flavored with saffron or food coloring. Using milk along with water gives it a creamy texture and enhances the flavor. It’s often served during festivals, celebrations, and as an offering in temples.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rava (semolina)
  • 3/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1.5 cups water
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • 10-12 cashew nuts
  • 10-12 raisins
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • A few saffron strands or a pinch of orange food color

Preparation Steps

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon ghee in a pan. Fry cashews until golden, and fry raisins until they puff up. Set aside.
  2. In the same pan, roast rava on low flame until aromatic and slightly golden. Set aside.
  3. In a separate pot, boil 1.5 cups water and 1 cup milk together. Add saffron strands or food color to the mixture.
  4. Slowly add the hot milk-water mix to the roasted rava, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  5. Cook until the rava absorbs all the liquid and becomes soft and fluffy.
  6. Add sugar and stir well. It will loosen for a while—keep stirring until it thickens again.
  7. Gradually add ghee while mixing, until the mixture leaves the sides of the pan.
  8. Stir in cardamom powder, fried cashews, and raisins.
  9. Serve hot or warm. Enjoy your creamy, aromatic Kesari!
