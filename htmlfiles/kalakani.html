Kalakani

Kalakand is a rich and moist Indian sweet made primarily from milk and sugar, flavored with cardamom and garnished with dry fruits. Its soft, grainy texture and delicate taste make it a favorite during festivals and celebrations.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup crumbled paneer or chenna (optional for faster process)
  • 1/2 cup condensed milk (or 3/4 cup sugar)
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1 tbsp ghee (for greasing)
  • Chopped pistachios and almonds for garnish

Preparation Steps

  1. Boil milk in a heavy-bottomed pan and keep stirring until it reduces to half its volume.
  2. Add crumbled paneer (if using) and continue cooking on low flame until the mixture thickens and leaves the sides of the pan.
  3. Stir in condensed milk (or sugar) and cardamom powder, and cook until well blended and the mixture forms a soft fudge-like texture.
  4. Grease a plate or tray with ghee and pour the hot kalakand mixture into it. Flatten and level the surface.
  5. Sprinkle chopped nuts on top and gently press them in.
  6. Let it cool completely and then cut into squares.
  7. Store in the refrigerator and serve chilled or at room temperature.
