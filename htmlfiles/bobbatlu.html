Bobbatlu

Bobbatlu, also known as Puran Poli, is a traditional South Indian sweet flatbread made with a stuffing of jaggery and chana dal. Often prepared during festivals, it's roasted with ghee and loved for its soft texture and rich, sweet filling.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chana dal (Bengal gram)
  • 3/4 cup grated jaggery
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Water (as needed for kneading)
  • A pinch of salt
  • Ghee for roasting

Preparation Steps

  1. Soak chana dal for 1–2 hours, then pressure cook until soft. Drain well.
  2. Grind cooked dal with grated jaggery to a smooth paste. Add cardamom powder.
  3. Prepare dough using flour, oil, a pinch of salt, and water. Let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough and filling into equal-sized balls.
  5. Roll a dough ball slightly, place a portion of filling in the center, seal it, and roll flat like a roti.
  6. Heat a tawa and roast the bobbatlu on both sides using ghee until golden brown.
  7. Serve warm with extra ghee on top. Delicious and festive!
