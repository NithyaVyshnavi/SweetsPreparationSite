Rasagulla

Rasgulla is a soft and spongy Indian dessert made from fresh chenna (curdled milk), shaped into balls, and boiled in a light sugar syrup. It's a popular Bengali sweet, known for its juicy texture and mildly sweet flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 2–3 tablespoons lemon juice or vinegar (to curdle milk)
  • 1.5 cups sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 2–3 cardamom pods (optional, for aroma)
  • 1 teaspoon rose water or kewra water (optional)

Preparation Steps

  1. Boil the milk. Once it starts boiling, reduce heat and add lemon juice slowly while stirring.
  2. When milk curdles completely, turn off the heat. Strain the curds using a muslin cloth. Rinse under cold water to remove lemon flavor.
  3. Hang the cloth for 30 minutes to drain excess water, forming soft chenna.
  4. Knead the chenna gently for 8–10 minutes until smooth and no lumps remain.
  5. Divide and roll the mixture into smooth small balls (ensure no cracks).
  6. In a wide pot, boil 4 cups of water with 1.5 cups sugar and cardamom pods.
  7. Once boiling, add the chenna balls, cover, and cook on medium flame for 15 minutes. They will expand.
  8. Turn off heat and let them cool in syrup. Add rose or kewra water if desired.
  9. Refrigerate for a few hours and serve chilled. Enjoy soft, spongy rasgullas!
