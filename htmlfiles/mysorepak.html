Mysore Pak

Mysore Pak is a rich South Indian sweet made with gram flour, ghee, and sugar. It originated in the royal kitchens of Mysore and is known for its melt-in-mouth texture and golden color.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1.5 cups sugar
  • 1/2 cup water

Preparation Steps

  1. Sieve the besan and dry roast it lightly on low heat until aromatic. Keep aside.
  2. In a pan, mix sugar and water. Heat until it forms a one-string consistency syrup.
  3. Gradually add besan to the syrup while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  4. In a separate pan, heat ghee and keep adding it little by little to the mixture while stirring.
  5. Continue stirring and cooking on medium heat until the mixture thickens and starts leaving the sides of the pan.
  6. Quickly pour the mixture into a greased tray and level it out.
  7. Allow it to cool slightly, then cut into square or rectangular pieces.
  8. Let it set completely before serving. Enjoy the rich, ghee-filled Mysore Pak!
