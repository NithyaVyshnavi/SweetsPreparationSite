Malpuri

Malpuri (also known as Malpua) is a traditional Indian sweet pancake, deep-fried and soaked in sugar syrup. This version is enriched with kova , which gives it a soft and rich texture, making it a festive favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/2 cup milk (adjust as needed)
  • Oil or ghee for deep frying
  • 1 cup grated kova (mawa/khoya)
  • Chopped dry fruits (optional)

Preparation Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix maida, sooji, sugar, cardamom powder, and milk to form a smooth batter. Rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pan on medium flame.
  3. Pour a ladle of batter on the hot oil like a small pancake (malpuri shape) and deep fry till golden on both sides.
  4. Remove and drain excess oil using tissue paper.
  5. Dip the malpuri briefly in light sugar syrup for added sweetness.
  6. Take a spoonful of grated kova, place it in the center of the fried malpuri, and fold it gently like a half-moon. Press edges softly to hold.
  7. Garnish with chopped dry fruits and serve warm.
