Bellam Gavvalu

Bellam Gavvalu is a traditional Andhra sweet made with wheat flour or maida shaped into small shells (gavvalu), deep-fried, and coated in thick jaggery syrup. These crunchy and sweet morsels are especially made during festivals like Sankranti and Diwali.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida) or wheat flour
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (for dough)
  • A pinch of salt
  • Water (as needed to make dough)
  • Oil (for deep frying)
  • 3/4 cup grated jaggery (bellam)
  • 1/4 cup water (for syrup)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder

Preparation Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, salt, and ghee. Add water gradually to make a smooth, stiff dough.
  2. Pinch small portions of dough and press each on a gavvalu board or back of a fork to get shell-like shapes.
  3. Heat oil in a pan and deep-fry the shaped gavvalu on medium flame until golden and crisp.
  4. In another pan, melt jaggery with 1/4 cup water to make a thick syrup. Add cardamom powder and mix well.
  5. Add the fried gavvalu into the hot jaggery syrup and stir quickly so they are well-coated.
  6. Transfer to a plate and let them cool down. They harden slightly and turn deliciously chewy and crunchy.
  7. Store in an airtight container and enjoy for days!
