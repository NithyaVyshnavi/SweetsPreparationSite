Jilebi

Jilebi (or Jalebi) is a crispy, juicy Indian dessert made by deep-frying fermented batter in spiral shapes and soaking them in sugar syrup. It’s known for its bright orange color, sticky texture, and sweet-tangy flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tablespoons cornflour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 cup yogurt (curd)
  • Water (as needed for thick batter)
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup water (for syrup)
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemon juice
  • Saffron strands or orange food color (optional)
  • Ghee or oil for frying

Preparation Steps

  1. Mix maida, cornflour, baking soda, and yogurt. Add water and whisk into a thick smooth batter. Let it ferment for 8–12 hours.
  2. Prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water until one-string consistency. Add lemon juice and saffron or food color if desired.
  3. Heat ghee or oil in a flat pan. Pour batter into a piping bag or ketchup bottle with a nozzle.
  4. Pipe spirals into hot oil and fry on low-medium flame until golden and crispy.
  5. Immediately transfer to warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for 2–3 minutes.
  6. Serve hot and juicy jilebis!
