Boondi Laddu

Boondi Laddu is a festive Indian sweet made from tiny fried droplets of gram flour (boondi), soaked in sugar syrup and shaped into laddus. It's especially popular during Diwali, weddings, and other celebrations.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed for batter)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water (for syrup)
  • 2–3 crushed cardamom pods
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil (for greasing)
  • Oil or ghee for deep frying
  • 2 tbsp chopped cashews and raisins (optional)

Preparation Steps

  1. Mix besan with water to make a smooth, flowing batter without lumps.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Hold a perforated ladle above the oil and pour batter over it to form small droplets (boondi).
  3. Fry the boondi until light golden. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. In a separate pan, boil sugar and water until it forms a sticky one-string consistency syrup.
  5. Add cardamom and optional fried cashews/raisins to the syrup. Turn off heat.
  6. Immediately add fried boondi to the hot syrup and mix well. Let it soak for a few minutes.
  7. While still warm, grease your palms with ghee and shape the mixture into laddus.
  8. Let them cool and set. Store in an airtight container.
